Bringing Healthcare to Rural Kenya

Limited access to healthcare in rural areas leads to high mortality rates from treatable conditions like malaria and maternal health issues.

About Us

We deploy mobile health clinics equipped with basic diagnostic tools and staffed by trained community health workers to provide vaccinations, prenatal care, and health education, aiming to serve rural communities in Kenya.

Our Services

Vaccinations

We provide essential vaccines to protect children and adults from diseases like measles, polio, and malaria, ensuring healthier communities.

Prenatal Care

Our clinics offer prenatal check-ups, nutritional advice, and safe delivery support to expectant mothers in rural areas.

Health Education

We educate communities on hygiene, disease prevention, and family planning to empower them with knowledge for better health.

Disease Screening

We conduct screenings for common rural diseases such as tuberculosis, HIV, and diabetes to enable early detection and treatment.

Emergency First Aid

Our teams are trained to provide immediate first aid for injuries and acute conditions, stabilizing patients until further care is available.

Nutritional Support

We offer nutritional assessments and supplements to combat malnutrition, especially among children and pregnant women.

Frequently Asked Questions

What services do the clinics provide?

Our clinics offer vaccinations, prenatal care, health education, disease screening, emergency first aid, and nutritional support to rural communities.

How can I volunteer?

Contact us through the form in the "Get Involved" section, and we'll reach out with opportunities.

Where do donations go?
